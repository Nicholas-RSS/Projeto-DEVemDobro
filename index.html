Dharma

Dharma means truth, the right way of living, and human behaviors considered necessary for the order of things in the world. On a grander scale, it refers to the cosmic law or rules that created the Universe from chaos

Artha

Artha is the security of having the material comfort you need to live in the world with ease. While some people think that to be spiritual means to be poor, Artha is not about rejecting the world, but being content with the things you own. It’s to live skillfully in a world of material objects that exist for your benefit.

The 4 Purusharthas

The Purusharthas are the inherent values of the Universe, the blueprint for human fulfillment. Working with them helps you create a satisfyingly balanced, meaningful life at the deepest and most holistic level. Purushatha means “for the purpose of the Self.

Kama

The desire for pleasure is what drives human behavior. A life without pleasure and enjoyment is hollow and empty. Kama relates to this pleasure, which can be sensuality, but is also art, music, beauty, love, intimacy, affection, fellowship, and kindness—it’s what brings a sense of delight to your life.

Moksha

When you live your Dharma, fully supported by Artha and Kama, Moksha or the final liberation dawns. Moksha is your true nature—it’s who you really are, is seen as a final release from life’s illusion.
